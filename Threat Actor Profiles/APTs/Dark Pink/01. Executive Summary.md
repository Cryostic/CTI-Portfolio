# 01. Executive Summary 

<p>
  First publicly identified in January 2023 by Group-IB researchers, Dark Pink (also tracked as Saaiwc Group) has been operational since at least mid-2021 based on evidences.
  The group conducts targeted cyber espionage campaigns primarily against military, government, and defense-adjacent organizations across the Asia-Pacific and Southeast Asian region.
  European entities with diplomatic ties to ASEAN Nations has also been subjected to occasional targeting
</p>

<p>
  Dark Pink is notable for deploying an exclusively custom-developed malware toolkit and for its highly unconventional use of telegram as a Command-and-Control (C2) channel.
  The group also employs <b>Event Triggered Execution via File Type Association (T1546.001)</b> which is a rarely documented technique, this distinguishes their 
  tradecraft most known APT Actors.
</p>

<p>
  The group's strategic targeting of military organizations, government ministries, and national defense-adjacent bodies in ASEAN Countries suggests a state-backed 
  mandate for geopolitical intelligence collection. Their interest in diplomatic communication between ASEAN and European Nations indicates that is both regional and internationally scoped.
  Dark Pink deliberately and effectively attempt to blend malicious traffic into legitimate baselines by the use Telegram for C2, a consumer messaging paltform.
  This makes detection significally harder for organiazations that do not monitor or restrict messaging application traffic.
</p>

<p>
  Dark Pink has the ability to:
  <ul>
    <li> Infect and propagate throught USB-connected devices </li>
    <li> Access and harvest data from desktop messaging application (Telegram, Viber, Zalo) </li>
    <li> Remain operational with persistent, rotating Github-hosted payloads </li>
  </ul>

  These abilities underscores a level of operational discipline that exceeds what one would expect from a newly emerged or ammature threat actor.x` 
</p>
