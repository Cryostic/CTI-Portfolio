# 01. Executive Summary

<p>
  Operational since 2019, Earth Lusca is a Chinese state-linked Advanced Persistent Threat group first formally named by Trend Micro back in January 2022.
  The group has been tracked under over a dozen aliases across the global Cyber Threat Intelligence community. Among these names are the most notable ones -
  FishMonger(ESET), RedHotel(Recorded Future), TAG-22(Insikt Group), and Aquatic Panda(Crowdstrike). Each of these aliases reflecting the same underlying 
  cluster observed through different research telemetry window.

  In March 2025, U.S. DOJ indictments of I-SOON (Anxun Information Technology) employees formally confirmed that FishMonger / Earth Lusca's operational identity in ESET's tracking, 
  the group is the espionage arm of I-SOON, a **Chinese private contractor operating on behalf of China's Ministry of Public Security (MPS) and Ministry of State Security (MSS)**. 
  This confirmation, independently corroborated by ESET's forensic analysis, elevates Earth Lusca's attribution to the highest confidence level.
</p>

<p>
  Earth Lusca's I-SOON contractor model has a specific implication that distinguishes it from state-apparatus actors like APT40 (directly operated by Hainan State Security Department): 
  **I-SOON operates as a hackers-for-hire contractor accepting tasking from multiple Chinese government agencies**. This means Earth Lusca's target set is not governed by a single coherent intelligence mandate 
  but by the diversity of agencies purchasing I-SOON's services. This explains the group's unusually broad geographic and sectoral scope. Government entities from Taiwan to France, Catholic charities in Hungary, 
  online gambling platforms in Southeast Asia, and COVID-19 research organizations in the United States have all been victims.
</p>

<p>
  Earth Lusca's preference for public-facing server exploitation and N-day vulnerabilities (Fortinet, Microsoft Exchange, Zimbra, GitLab, Telerik UI, Log4j) makes **patch velocity** the primary defensive variable, exactly as with APT40. 
  The financial motivation element means that even organizations outside the traditional government/defense APT target set particularly cryptocurrency exchanges, online gaming platforms, and financial technology firms 
  should treat Earth Lusca as an active threat.
</p>
