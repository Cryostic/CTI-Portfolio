# 04. Campaign History

<h2>Campaign Timeline</h2>

<h3>Foundation & Regional Focus (2012–2019)</h3>
<p>Defining characteristic: South Asian Government and Military targeting; commodity RATs; Early Custom Tools</p>

```
2012
└── Operational activity begins
    └── Primary focus: Pakistan Government and Military Entities
    └── Initial toolset: commodity RATs (AndroRAT for mobile; early custom loaders)
    
2015–2017
└── Expansion to China diplomatic targets, Nepal, Sri Lanka
└── Heavy use of spear-phishing with Microsoft Office document lures
└── AndroidRAT campaigns targeting Pakistani and South Asian mobile users
└── Pakistan-themed lures: government advisories, military notices
└── China-themed lures: diplomatic correspondence, policy documents

APRIL 12, 2018
└── Kaspersky formally names the group "SideWinder" in APT Trends Q1 2018
    └── First formal public CTI profile; previously untracked publicly
    └── CrowdStrike assigns parallel designation "Razor Tiger"
    └── Group-IB begins independent tracking

2018–2019
└── Continued Pakistan/South Asia Government targeting
└── Chinese Diplomatic Entities targeted
└── Network Infrastructure begins to professionalize: multiple domains, subdomains
└── Multiple South Asian campaigns across Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Afghanistan
```

<h3> Volume Surge & WarHawk Deployment (2022–2023)</h3>
<p>Defining characteristic: Highest Operational Tempo; WarHawk Backdoor; Server-Side Polymorphism; 1,000+ Intrusions</p>

```
2022 : KASPERSKY VOLUME SURGE DOCUMENTED
├── Kaspersky logs over 1,000 SideWinder intrusions in 18 months
│   └── This is extraordinary for a regional APT - Mustang Panda-level volume
├── Group operating against targets across South and Southeast Asia simultaneously
└── "SideWinder.AntiBot.Script" first documented
    └── Server-Side Script: redirects victims for initial file download
    └── Filters non-target IPs from receiving malicious content
    └── Marks first formal documentation of SideWinder's geofencing capability

LATE NOVEMBER 2022 : PAKISTAN CAMPAIGN / WARHAWK DISCOVERED (Zscaler)
├── BlackBerry documents Pakistan government organization targeting
├── New backdoor discovered: "WarHawk"
│   ├── Modular architecture: Download & Execute, Remote Shell, File Manager
│   ├── Delivers Cobalt Strike as final payload (off-the-shelf C2)
│   ├── Pakistan Standard Time check: ONLY executes on PST-configured systems
│   │   └── INTELLIGENCE SIGNIFICANCE: geo-check built into malware code itself
│   ├── Server-based polymorphism: payload morphs to evade static detection
│   └── ISO files hosted on Pakistan's National Electric Power Regulatory Authority
│       website - legitimate hosting infrastructure abused
├── Lures: Pakistan Cabinet Division advisories; official-looking government documents
└── Infrastructure: reuses known SideWinder domains (confirming attribution)

EARLY 2023 : TURKEY IN SCOPE
└── BlackBerry identifies Turkey emerging as targeting priority
    └── First documented European-adjacent government targeting
    └── Signals intent to follow diplomatic relationships beyond South Asia
    
2023 : CONTINUED SOUTH ASIA DOMINANCE
└── Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh remain primary targets
└── Toolset refinement continues; multi-stage loaders evolving
└── Infrastructure professionalization: 400+ domain ecosystem documented
```

<h3> Sector Expansion - Maritime & Nuclear (2024)</h3>
<p>Defining characteristic: First Maritime Targeting; Nuclear Sector Emergence; StealerBot revealed; Geographic Explosion</p>

```
JULY 2024 : MARITIME TARGETING FIRST FORMAL DOCUMENTATION (BlackBerry)
├── SecurityWeek headlines: "Indian APT Targeting Mediterranean Ports and Maritime Facilities"
├── BlackBerry Threat Research formally documents maritime sector campaigns
├── Targets:
│   ├── Egypt: Port authorities, maritime infrastructure
│   ├── Djibouti: Logistics companies (Indian Ocean maritime hub)
│   ├── UAE: Maritime and logistics
│   ├── Bangladesh: Naval and maritime entities
│   ├── Cambodia: Port logistics
│   └── Vietnam: Port authorities
├── Lure themes: maritime infrastructure documents; port authority communications
└── Infection chain: spear-phishing DOCX --> DLL side-loading → implant

OCTOBER 2024 : STEALERBOT REVELATION (Kaspersky)
├── Kaspersky publishes definitive technical analysis: "SideWinder APT's 
│   Post-Exploitation Framework Analysis"
├── StealerBot disclosed for the first time
│   ├── Custom .NET-based modular implant; NEVER stored on filesystem
│   ├── Loaded entirely in memory by Backdoor Loader
│   ├── Orchestrator module manages all plugins via C2 communication
│   └── Observed plugins documented for first time (see Section 06)
├── Assessment revised: SideWinder is NOT a low-skilled actor
├── Targets in this campaign period:
│   ├── Bangladesh, Djibouti, Jordan, Malaysia, Maldives, Myanmar, Nepal
│   ├── Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Sri Lanka, Turkey, UAE
│   └── Diplomatic entities: Afghanistan, France, China, India, Indonesia, Morocco
├── Nuclear sector first observed: "some lure documents concerned nuclear 
│   power plants and nuclear energy agencies" - Kaspersky
└── Geographic scope: widest ever documented for SideWinder
```
