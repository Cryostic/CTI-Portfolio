# 01. Executive Summary

<p>
  SideWinder is a prolific, India-nexus Advanced Persistent Threat group assessed to have been active since at least 2012 and formally named by Kaspersky in April 2018. 
  The group has historically focused on Government, Military, and Diplomatic targets across South and Southeast Asia particularly in Pakistan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Afghanistan, and China 
  but has undergone a dramatic strategic expansion since 2022, now running simultaneous campaigns across Asia, the Middle East, Africa, and Europe.

  For most of its documented history, SideWinder was characterized as a relatively unsophisticated actor due to its reliance on old, publicly-known Microsoft Office exploits. 
  This assessment was fundamentally revised in October 2024 when Kaspersky revealed StealerBot - a sophisticated, fully custom, in-memory modular espionage toolkit that had been 
  operating at the post-compromise stage for years, largely invisible to security researchers. The group's true capabilities are substantially greater than previously understood.
</p>

<p>
  SideWinder's targeting of Maritime Logistics Operators, Port Authorities, Nuclear Energy Agencies, and Nuclear Regulatory bodies alongside its traditional Government and Military Intelligence Mandate Signals 
  an expansion of its tasking requirements either by directive or by autonomous operational broadening. Organizations in the maritime and nuclear sectors in South Asia, the Middle East (Egypt, Djibouti, UAE), Africa (Mozambique),
  and Southeast Asia (Bangladesh, Cambodia, Vietnam) are now fully in scope. The group's 1,000+ documented intrusions in an 18-month window (2022–2023) make it one of the highest-volume APT actors in the APAC region.
</p>
