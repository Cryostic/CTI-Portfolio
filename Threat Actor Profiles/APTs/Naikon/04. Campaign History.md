# 04. Campaign History

<h2>Campaign Timeline</h2>

<h3>Establishment & First Exposure (2010-2015)</h3>
<p>Defining Characteristics: South China Sea ASEAN targeting; PlugX/XSAgent; five-year undetected operation; first named operator exposure</p>

```
2010
└── Naikon APT operational activity assessed to begin
    └── PLA Unit 78020, Kunming, Yunnan Province
    └── Mandate: ASEAN geopolitical intelligence; South China Sea situational awareness
    └── Initial targets: top-level government agencies of ASEAN South China Sea claimants

2010–2015 : FIRST OPERATIONAL ERA (Undetected)
├── Sustained espionage operations across the South China Sea littoral states
├── Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, Myanmar, Thailand, Brunei
├── Targets: Ministries of Foreign Affairs, Science & Technology, military commands
├── ASEAN Secretariat targeted for pan-regional intelligence
├── United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) targeted
├── Australian government contacts used as attack vector (early government-to-gov chain)
├── Tools: XSAgent (primary backdoor), PlugX variants, custom loaders
├── Infrastructure: "greensky27.vicp.net" operator-linked domain active during this period
│   └── This domain becomes the anchor for later attribution
└── APT-on-APT incident: Naikon mistakenly phished a "Hellsing" group member
    └── Hellsing retaliates by targeting Naikon's own infrastructure
    └── Documented by Kaspersky — provides forensic visibility into both actors

APRIL 2015 : KASPERSKY "NAIKON APT" REPORT
├── Kaspersky publishes first comprehensive Naikon technical report
├── Formally names the group "Naikon"
├── Documents infrastructure, TTPs, and victim geography
└── Group goes "apparently dark" — likely in response to public exposure

MAY 2015 : THREATCONNECT + DGI ATTRIBUTION REPORT
├── "The Naikon APT: China's PLA Unit 78020"
├── Attributes Naikon to Chengdu Military Region Second TRB (Unit 78020)
├── Names operator: Ge Xing ("GreenSky27"), PRC national, Kunming
├── Documents 8 overlapping "pattern of life" correlations between Ge Xing
│   and the greensky27.vicp.net C2 domain
├── Physical location confirmed: "routine physical access to PLA compound" in Kunming
├── PLA specialization confirmed: Ge Xing specializes in Southeast Asian politics
└── Report published same day as Chinese President Xi Jinping's U.S. state visit
    (timing noted as potentially deliberate diplomatic signaling by researchers)
```

<h3>Apparent Dormancy --> Reloaded Campaign (2015–2020)</h3>
<p>Defining Characteristics: 4-year apparent hiatus; Aria-Body developed; accelerating 2019 activity; government-to-government attack chain pioneered</p>

```
2015–2019 : THE "DARK PERIOD" (Apparent Dormancy)
├── No publicly visible Naikon campaign activity following Kaspersky/ThreatConnect exposure
├── REALITY: Group almost certainly retooling and improving operational security
├── Development period: Aria-Body backdoor developed (first use assessed ~2018)
│   └── Aria-Body is a substantial capability upgrade from XSAgent
└── Check Point 2020 retrospective: "the group has not only been active for the 
    past five years, but has also accelerated its cyber espionage activities in 
    2019 and early 2020"

JUNE 2019 : NAIKON RESURFACES (Aria-Body Era Begins)
├── Activity accelerates significantly in 2019
├── Government-to-government pivot attack chain first documented:
│   └── Step 1: Compromise Government A
│   └── Step 2: Use Government A's documents, contacts, and email to attack Government B
│   └── Step 3: Use compromised Government B's servers as C2 for Step 4 attacks
│   └── This technique exploits diplomatic trust relationships between governments
├── Aria-Body RAT becomes primary implant
│   └── First surfaced ~2018; continuously refined through 2020
│   └── Screenshot capability, keylogging, file operations, reverse proxy
├── Targets confirmed: Australia, Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand, Myanmar, Brunei
├── Philippine Government Dept. of Science & Technology server used as C2 (documented)
└── Australia targeted via infected document sent FROM a compromised APAC embassy

MAY 6, 2020 : CHECK POINT "CYBER ESPIONAGE RELOADED" REPORT
├── Check Point Research publishes comprehensive post-2015 analysis
├── Documents 5-year campaign that "went under the radar"
├── Aria-Body fully documented for first time
├── Government-to-government attack methodology formally described:
│   └── "Using exploits attributed to lots of APT groups, and uniquely using 
│       their victims' servers as command and control centers"
└── Covers: Australia, Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand, Myanmar, Brunei
```

<h3>Military Targeting & Telecom Pivot (2019–2021)</h3>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Bitdefender military organizations campaign; RainyDay + Nebulae combination; DeadRinger simultaneous multi-group telecom operation</p>

```
JUNE 2019 - MARCH 2021 : MILITARY ORGANIZATIONS CAMPAIGN (Bitdefender)
├── Naikon specifically targets military organizations in Southeast Asia
├── Marks FIRST documented dedicated military targeting (vs. diplomatic/government)
├── Tool evolution: Aria-Body → RainyDay (September 2020 transition)
│   └── "Starting with September 2020, the threat actors included the 
│       RainyDay backdoor in their toolkit" (Bitdefender)
├── Nebulae introduced as dedicated persistence backdoor
│   └── Nebulae + RainyDay combination: dual-backdoor for resilient persistence
├── DLL sideloading via legitimate trusted applications:
│   └── ARO Tutorial, VirusScan McAfee, Sandboxie, Mobile Popup Application
├── Credential theft: ProcDump (LSASS dump) + modified Mimikatz
└── APRIL 29, 2021: Bitdefender publishes "NAIKON: Traces from a Military 
    Cyber-Espionage Operation" — group activity slows following exposure

Q4 2020 - Q1 2021 : OPERATION DEADRINGER: CLUSTER B (Cybereason)
├── Cybereason discovers three-group simultaneous telecom operator targeting
├── Naikon operates as "Cluster B" (confirmed via Nebulae backdoor)
├── Targets: Major telecommunications companies in Southeast Asia
│   └── All companies targeted by multiple Chinese APT groups simultaneously
│   └── SoftCell (Cluster A) and Group-3390/APT27 (Cluster C) also present
│   └── Multiple groups found ON THE SAME ENDPOINTS simultaneously
├── Naikon's Cluster B activities:
│   ├── Nebulae backdoor via DLL sideloading (chrome_frame_helper.exe)
│   ├── EnrollLogger: custom keylogger targeting high-privilege accounts
│   │   └── "Previously undocumented" — unique to Naikon in DeadRinger
│   ├── PAExec: lateral movement
│   ├── WMI: lateral movement and reconnaissance
│   └── Modified Mimikatz + ProcDump: credential theft
├── Goal: Call record data; network topology; subscriber metadata
└── AUGUST 2021: Cybereason publishes DeadRinger — Naikon activity slows
```

<h3>The 2022 Resurgence & Geographic Pivot (2022–Present)</h3>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Third resurgence; WAF procurement lure; Central/South Asia telecom expansion; new PlugX variant; near-2-year dwell time documented</p>

```
2022 : THIRD RESURGENCE - WAF PROCUREMENT LURE CAMPAIGN (Cluster25)
├── Cluster25 identifies new Naikon campaign
├── Initial access method: Weaponized DOCX via spear-phishing
│   └── Document: "Tender Documents for Centralized Procurement of 
│       Web Application Firewall (WAF) Equipment of China Mobile 2022–2024"
│       └── Written in Chinese — notable for ASEAN-focused actor
│   └── VBA macro extracts code from Subject/Comments metadata fields
│       → Writes to %TEMP% folder for execution
├── Open-source pen-testing tools combined with custom backdoors
└── SIGNIFICANCE: Third documented resurgence; retooled after DeadRinger exposure

2022 - DECEMBER 2024 : TELECOM/MANUFACTURING CENTRAL+SOUTH ASIA CAMPAIGN
(Documented retrospectively by Cisco Talos, September 2025)
├── Campaign active: late 2022 through at least December 2024
│   └── "One of the log files demonstrated successful persistence within the 
│       victim's environment, recording activity from late 2022 through 
│       December 2024 — spanning nearly two years of ongoing compromise"
├── GEOGRAPHIC PIVOT: Central Asia and South Asia (NOT traditional ASEAN focus)
│   └── Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan telecom companies confirmed (Talos)
│   └── Overlaps with BackdoorDiplomacy targeting in South Asia
├── NEW PlugX VARIANT deployed:
│   ├── Configuration file uses RainyDay structure (not standard PlugX format)
│   ├── "Strongly suggesting the threat actor has access to PlugX source code"
│   │   and has customized it to match Naikon's operational conventions
│   ├── Same XOR-RC4-RtlDecompressBuffer algorithm as RainyDay and Turian
│   ├── Same RC4 keys across all three malware families
│   ├── DLL search order hijacking via Mobile Popup Application (Quick Heal)
│   │   (same legitimate app used in previous RainyDay deployments)
│   └── Embedded keylogger functionality in PlugX payload itself
├── BackdoorDiplomacy overlap assessment (Talos):
│   └── "These malware families suggest that Naikon and BackdoorDiplomacy 
│       are the same group, or that both are sourcing tools from the same vendor"
└── SEPTEMBER 25, 2025: Cisco Talos publishes DLL search order hijacking report

SEPTEMBER 2025 : CURRENT OPERATIONAL STATUS
└── Naikon confirmed ACTIVE through at least late 2024 (Talos keylogger logs)
└── Picus Security (May 2026): CVE-2025-21590 (Junos OS) in current exploit inventory
└── Group remains fully operational; no indicators of pause or cessation
```
