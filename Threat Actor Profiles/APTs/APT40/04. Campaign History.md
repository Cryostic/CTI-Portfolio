# 04. Campaign History

<h2>Campaign Timeline by Operational Era</h2>

<h3>Establishment & Naval Focus (2009-2016)</h3>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Naval and Maritime Intelligence Collection.</p>

```
2009
└── Operational activity assessed to begin
    └── Hainan State Security Department establishes CNE capability
    └── Hainan Xiandun Technology Development Company serves as cover
    └── Initial focus: U.S. naval technology and maritime engineering

2011 (Per DOJ Indictment)
└── First documented malicious activity per indictment period
    └── Targets: U.S. companies, universities, government entities
    └── Global campaign spanning "dozens of companies, universities, 
        and government entities" (DOJ language)

2013
└── FireEye/Mandiant begin tracking TEMP.Periscope (pre-naming)
└── Campaigns confirmed: maritime targets, defense, aviation, 
    chemicals, research/education, government, technology
└── Target geography: U.S., Western Europe, South China Sea region

DECEMBER 2016
└── China's PLAN seizes U.S. Navy Unmanned Underwater Vehicle (UUV)
    in South China Sea
    └── APT40 naval intelligence collection provides strategic context
        for PRC assertiveness in maritime domain
    └── This event is cited by Mandiant as illustrative of APT40's 
        broader strategic mandate
```

<h2>Public Emergence & Cambodia Election (2017-2018)</h2>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Proofpoint "Leviathan" naming; Cambodia Election Operation; BRI-Partner targeting expands</p>

```
OCTOBER 2017 : PROOFPOINT "LEVIATHAN" REPORT
├── Proofpoint becomes first vendor to publish formal analysis
├── Names group "Leviathan"
├── Documents sustained maritime and defense spear-phishing campaigns
├── Confirms targeting of U.S. and European maritime/defense sectors
└── Introduces BADFLICK, AIRBREAK, HOMEFRY toolset to public record

2017–2018 : BRI PARTNER NATION CAMPAIGN
├── Targeting expands to BRI-critical nations:
│   Cambodia, Belgium, Germany, Hong Kong, Philippines,
│   Malaysia, Norway, Saudi Arabia, Switzerland, UK
└── Front company recruitment: Hainan Xiandun posts job ads seeking
    Cambodian, Indonesian, Vietnamese translators (March–April 2018)
    └── INTELLIGENCE SIGNIFICANCE: language hiring precedes targeting surge

JULY 11, 2018 : CAMBODIA ELECTION OPERATION
├── FireEye documents targeting of Cambodian government entities
│   responsible for overseeing elections
├── Opposition political figures targeted
├── Timing: Precisely before July 29, 2018 National Assembly election
└── Goal: Electoral intelligence; political outcome monitoring

2018 : DOJ INDICTMENT PERIOD ENDS
└── Indictment documents activity from 2011 through 2018
    └── Confirmed victims: U.S., Austria, Cambodia, Germany, 
        Malaysia, UK, Norway (and others)
    └── Industries: aviation, defense, education, government, 
        healthcare, biopharmaceutical, maritime
```

<h2>Formal Attribution & COVID-Era Expansion (2019-2021)</h2>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Mandiant APT40 naming; DOJ Indictment; COVID-19 Vaccine Research Targeting; ProxyPogon Mass Exploitation</p>

```
MARCH 4, 2019 : MANDIANT FORMALLY NAMES APT40
├── FireEye publishes "APT40: Examining a China-Nexus Espionage Actor"
├── First formal public attribution to Hainan, PRC
├── Documents 2013–2019 activity comprehensively
├── Naval modernization thesis formally stated:
│   "We believe APT40's emphasis on maritime issues and naval technology 
│    ultimately support China's ambition to establish a blue-water navy"
└── Comprehensive malware ecosystem documented for first time

2019 : INTRUSION TRUTH EXPOSURE
├── Anonymous research group Intrusion Truth publishes series of reports
├── Identifies 13 Hainan-based front companies linked to APT40 operations
├── Hainan Xiandun identified by name
├── FireEye and Kaspersky researchers publicly corroborate the findings
└── Physical addresses, LinkedIn profiles, recruitment ads compiled

2019 : U.S. NAVY CONTRACTOR BREACH
└── APT40 infiltrates contractors involved in submarine propulsion 
    and undersea warfare research
    └── "Sea Dragon" project material reported breached by Washington Post
    └── 614 gigabytes of data reportedly stolen

2020 : COVID-19 VACCINE RESEARCH TARGETING
├── APT40 joins other PRC actors in targeting COVID-19 vaccine research
├── Targets: pharmaceutical companies, biomedical research institutions
├── Biomedical sector elevated to high priority (confirmed in DOJ indictment)
└── Healthcare data remains in scope for intelligence collection

MARCH 2021 : PROXYLOGON MASS EXPLOITATION
├── APT40 linked to wave of attacks targeting 250,000+ Microsoft Exchange servers
├── ProxyLogon vulnerability (CVE-2021-26855) exploited at massive scale
├── Web shells deployed across victim environments globally
└── DEFINES the group's infrastructure exploitation preference over phishing
    └── This event becomes the central example in July 2024 advisory

JULY 19, 2021 : U.S. DOJ INDICTMENT (UNSEALED)
├── Four APT40 operators indicted:
│   ├── Ding Xiaoyang — MSS HSSD intelligence officer (handler)
│   ├── Zhu Yunmin — MSS HSSD intelligence officer (handler)
│   ├── Cheng Qingmin — MSS HSSD intelligence officer (handler)
│   └── Wu Shurong — Hainan Xiandun employee (operator/developer)
├── Front company formally exposed: Hainan Xiandun Technology Development Co.
├── "PRC government paid" — DOJ language confirms state funding
├── FBI issues wanted posters for all four individuals
└── CISA publishes companion advisory (AA21-200A) with IOC package

JULY 19, 2021 : CISA / FBI JOINT ADVISORY (AA21-200A)
└── Comprehensive TTP documentation
└── IOC package published (network artifacts, file hashes, C2 patterns)
└── Emphasis on VPN credential targeting and web shell persistence
```

<h2>Post-Indictment Persistence & SOHO Botnet Doctrine (2022-2024)</h2>
<p>Defining Characteristics: Operational Continuity Despite Indictment; SOHO Router Weaponization; N-day Rapid Exploitation; New Zealand Parliamentary Breach</p>

```
2021 : NEW ZEALAND PARLIAMENTARY BREACH
├── APT40 compromises Parliamentary Counsel Office and Parliamentary Service
├── New Zealand NCSC detects and contains activity quickly
└── Attribution withheld publicly for 3 years (disclosed March 2024)

MID-AUGUST 2022 : AUSTRALIA CASE STUDY #1 (per ASD/ACSC Advisory)
├── ACSC notifies an Australian organization of confirmed compromise
├── Malicious IP affiliated with Chinese state-sponsored group detected
├── Compromised device: probable SOHO router of a small business/home user
├── Attack pattern: SOHO router used as last-hop redirector → victim network
└── SIGNIFICANCE: First formally documented Australian SOHO botnet case

APRIL 2022 : AUSTRALIA CASE STUDY #2 (per ASD/ACSC Advisory)
└── Web shell discovered on Australian organization with April 2022 creation date
    └── Demonstrates web shell persistence maintained for months before
        organization was even aware of the compromise
    └── ACSC advisory documents subsequent lateral movement and credential theft

2022–2023 : GLOBAL N-DAY EXPLOITATION SURGE
├── APT40 exploits high-profile CVEs within hours of public disclosure:
│   ├── CVE-2021-44228 (Log4j / Log4Shell) — mass exploitation
│   ├── CVE-2021-26084 (Atlassian Confluence RCE)
│   ├── CVE-2021-31207 (Microsoft Exchange)
│   ├── CVE-2021-34523 (Microsoft Exchange ProxyShell)
│   └── CVE-2021-34473 (Microsoft Exchange ProxyShell)
├── ASD/ACSC: "APT40 possesses the capability to rapidly transform and adapt 
│   exploit PoCs of new vulnerabilities and immediately utilise them against 
│   target networks possessing the infrastructure of the associated vulnerability"
└── SOHO router botnet operationally mature; used as primary last-hop C2 relay

AUGUST 2023 : PAPUA NEW GUINEA CAMPAIGN (Google TAG)
├── Google Threat Analysis Group tracks as "IslandDreams"
├── Spear-phishing campaign targeting PNG government officials
├── Novel delivery: multi-attachment email with exploit + decoy PDF + LNK file
├── LNK file loads malicious DLL from hard-coded IP or file-sharing site
├── Final payload: BoxRat — in-memory .NET backdoor using Dropbox API for C2
│   └── SIGNIFICANCE: Dropbox API as C2 channel is effectively unblockable 
│       without disrupting legitimate enterprise Dropbox usage
└── Pacific Island region formally enters APT40 target set

MARCH 26, 2024 : NEW ZEALAND FORMAL ATTRIBUTION
├── New Zealand GCSB Minister Judith Collins publicly attributes 2021 breach to APT40
├── "The NCSC completed a robust technical assessment following a compromise 
│   of the Parliamentary Counsel Office and the Parliamentary Service in 2021"
├── "Has attributed this activity to a PRC state-sponsored group known as APT40"
├── Disclosure timed to coincide with U.S./UK sanctions against APT31 operators
└── New Zealand confirms no sensitive data was compromised in the 2021 breach
    (quick detection and eviction before significant data exfiltration)

JULY 9, 2024 : LANDMARK EIGHT-NATION JOINT ADVISORY (AA24-190A)
├── Issued by: Australia (ASD/ACSC) + US (FBI/CISA) + UK (NCSC) + Canada (CSE/RCMP)
│             + Germany (BfV/BSI) + New Zealand (NCSC) + South Korea (NIS/KISA) 
│             + Japan (NPA/NISC)
├── Eight nations co-sign — unprecedented coalition for a single APT advisory
├── Key findings documented for public record:
│   ├── SOHO router botnet: confirmed standard operating procedure
│   ├── N-day exploitation: hours to days after PoC publication
│   ├── Infrastructure exploitation preferred over phishing
│   ├── Web shells: primary persistence mechanism
│   ├── Valid credential prioritization over malware dependency
│   └── Two Australian case studies shared with technical detail
├── ASD statement: "APT40 is actively performing regular reconnaissance 
│   on Australian networks of interest"
└── Advisory available at: cyber.gov.au/apt40-advisory
```

```
FEBRUARY 11, 2025 : SAMOA FORMAL ATTRIBUTION (SamCERT)
├── Samoa Computer Emergency Response Team (SamCERT) issues advisory
├── FIRST EVER Pacific Island nation to formally and publicly attribute
│   a cyberattack to a China-linked group
├── SamCERT: APT40 conducting "malicious cyber operations against 
│   government and key critical infrastructure systems" across Blue Pacific
├── Fileless malware and modified commodity malware documented
├── DLL side-loading, registry modifications, memory-based execution noted
├── Modified reverse proxies for C2 traffic disguise deployed
├── "APT40 gains long-term access to networks, executing reconnaissance 
│   and data theft operations over extended periods" — SamCERT
└── Advisory explicitly identifies Blue Pacific as active campaign theater

EARLY 2025 : PACIFIC ISLANDS FORUM SECRETARIAT
└── ABC (Australia) reports: Australian officials assessed Chinese-linked 
    group responsible for breach of Pacific Islands Forum Secretariat
    └── PIF represents the primary multilateral Pacific governance body
    └── Breach unattributed publicly by PIF itself

2025 : ONGOING GLOBAL OPERATIONS
├── Maritime sector: continued targeting globally
├── Defense industrial base: sustained U.S., European, Australian targets
├── Biomedical/pharmaceutical: continues in scope post-COVID
└── BRI partner nations: continued monitoring
    
2026 (CURRENT)
```
